# Review addendum — 2026-07-11

- **Directed by:** Brandon Dent, MD (project owner).
- **Clinical realism ratified:** an elevated troponin (0.31) with no new
  chest pain and an unchanged ECG is repeat-and-monitor with the ED physician
  reviewing — not an automatic bedside physician consult. Demo and journey
  content recast accordingly: the disclosure gap raises a **track-board
  notification**, the **RN acknowledges the result with the patient at the
  bedside** (a licensed provider; satisfies the documented-discussion
  requirement), the repeat troponin is the headline next step, and
  cardiology/observation appears only as trend-and-exam-dependent attributed
  general information.
- **Product framing ratified:** the journey panel is the ED room whiteboard
  that updates itself. Bedside whiteboards improve patients' knowledge of
  their plan (observational/QI evidence — Tan 2013; Goyal 2017 systematic
  review; no RCT exists) but go stale because updating is manual and the RN
  has too many tasks; deriving the board from chart state removes that labor
  instead of adding any.
- **Scope:** demonstration; single-reviewer; board governance still pending.
